History

The earliest known keyboard was part of a "five-needle" telegraph operation station. This was designed by Wheatstone and Cooke in 1836.
The five needles could point left or right to indicate the letter on the grid. A chorded keyboard invented by Stenotype in 1868, beacme popular and are still in use today.
There are many different telegraoh codes that exsited. One of the most popular telegraph code is the Baudot telegraph. In 1874, the five-bit Baudot telegraph code invented by Emile Baudot.
It was used for operators forming the codes manually.
The telegraph was designed for a matching 5-key chorded keyboard. The goal of the telegraph code is to achieve faster speed and low wear.
Each of the chords were specifically chosen so the most common characters used the simplest chords.
